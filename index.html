Wife

& Mother.

I have worked as a mentor to college students in Portland, Oregon ever since I graduated from college with a degree in Elementary Education. Since becoming a mother, I have increasingly desired to show my kids what it looks like to never stop growing and always challenge yourself. So I stepped into the world of software engineering. I'm eager to learn and always striving to be better. And I'm so proud to show my kids that you're never too old to try something new.

During the fall of 2020, I decided to dip my toes into the waters of web development. I immediately fell in love with the logic of code (usually) and also the creativity. I felt like I was able to bring my whole self to the table and contribute fully to projects I was working on. I decided that becoming a developer was worth quitting my job and attending Thinkful, all in the hopes of launching a brand new career. I am gaining confidence in JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap. I'm dedicated to learning all that I can, and I'm not afraid to ask questions or learn a better way. The last decade of my life has been working to develop my communication and I know that I will bring that strength to any team that I'm lucky enough to be a part of.

I've always enjoyed expressing my creativity through many platforms. When I was younger, I dedicated much of my free time to music. First it was classical piano, then I switched to primarily singing. In recent years I have entered into different art mediums from painting peg dolls to working with leather. I just invested in a pair of roller skates so that I can take a break from working on coding to get out and get active. I love learning new skills and am never satisfied with my current knowledge.
